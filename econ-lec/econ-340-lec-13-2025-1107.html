‚öôÔ∏è Market for Pollution Reduction: Carbon Market

  • Marginal Damage (MD) equals SMB ‚Äî each unit of reduction prevents one unit of harm to society.
  • Private MC (PMC) equals Social MC (SMC) ‚Äî there are no externalities in pollution reduction itself.
  • \(A\): Market outcome occurs where PMC = PMB ‚Äî with no incentive to reduce pollution.
  • \(B\): Optimal outcome occurs where SMC = SMB.

üí∏ Price Regulation: Carbon Tax

  • Government imposes a Pigouvian tax (œÑ) on each unit of pollution equal to its marginal damage (MD) ‚Äî for example, $100 per ton of CO‚ÇÇ.
  • Each plant reduces emissions as long as its marginal abatement cost (MC) is below œÑ, and pays the tax on remaining emissions.
  • Once MC = œÑ, further abatement would cost more than the tax, so the firm stops reducing.

üìè Quantity Regulation: Mandated Reduction

  • Regulation: Government simply mandates that firms reduce pollution by the optimal amount (R*).

Note

  • If MD is constant, this is manageable; but when MD declines with more reduction, the regulator must know both MD & MC curves to determine either the optimal tax or the optimal quantity.

üè≠ Heterogeneous Plants: Quantity with Flexibility

  • The government sets a total emissions cap (e.g., 200 permits) and lets firms trade them.
    • Each permit allows one unit of pollution. (Coase Theorem!)
  • A firm will:
    • Abate if its MC is less than the permit price ‚Üí cheaper to clean than to buy.
    • Buy permits if its MC is greater than the permit price ‚Üí cheaper to pollute than to clean.
    • Trading continues until no further mutually beneficial trades exist ‚Äî just like in a Coase bargain.

  • The result: the pollution target is met at the lowest total cost,

üå´Ô∏è Uncertainty About Costs of Reduction

  • So far, we assumed the government knows the true costs of pollution reduction.
  • In reality, marginal abatement costs (MC) are uncertain ‚Äî firms and regulators may not know the exact cost of cutting pollution.
  • Under uncertainty, the choice between price and quantity instruments depends on the shape of the marginal benefit (MD) curve.

Price vs. Quantity

  • In Practice:
    • The instrument choice depends on whether the government wants to get the amount of pollution reduction right or whether it wants to minimize costs to firms.
    • Quantity regulations ensure environmental protection, but at a variable cost to firms, while price regulations ensure the cost to the firms, but at a variable level of environmental protection
