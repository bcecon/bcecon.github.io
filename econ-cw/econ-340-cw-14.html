Classwork 15

Moral Hazard and the NFIP

Author

Byeong-Hak Choe

Published

December 8, 2025

Modified

December 1, 2025

Instructions

  • Answer parts (a)‚Äì(d) using average (expected) annual cost as your decision rule.
  • Show your calculations and briefly explain your reasoning in words.

Scenario

A homeowner is choosing between two locations:

  • Location S (Safe):
    • Annual housing cost (rent/mortgage, etc.): \(20{,}000\)
    • No flood risk.
  • Location F (Flood-prone):
    • Annual housing cost: \(18{,}000\)
    • Each year, there is a \(10\%\) chance of a flood causing \(100{,}000\) in damage to the house.

Assume the homeowner cares only about minimizing their average annual monetary cost.
You can think of:

Average annual cost \(=\) housing cost
\(\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad +\) average (expected) flood damage cost
\(\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad +\) any insurance premiums paid.

(a) No insurance

There is no flood insurance available.

  1. Compute the homeowner‚Äôs average annual cost in Location S.
  2. Compute the homeowner‚Äôs average annual cost in Location F (hint: use the probability of a flood and the size of the damage).
  3. Which location is cheaper on average?
  4. If the homeowner wants to minimize average annual cost, which location will they choose?


(b) NFIP with subsidized premium

Now suppose the government offers NFIP flood insurance in Location F:

  • The NFIP policy fully covers any flood damage in Location F.
  • The annual insurance premium is \(2{,}000\) (this is a subsidized, below-market premium).
  • If the homeowner lives in Location F, assume they buy this insurance.
  1. Compute the homeowner‚Äôs average annual cost in Location S (with NFIP available but not needed).
  2. Compute the homeowner‚Äôs average annual cost in Location F with subsidized NFIP insurance.
  3. Which location is now cheaper on average?
  4. How might this change the homeowner‚Äôs location choice compared to part (a)?


(c) NFIP with risk-based (actuarially fair) premium

Now suppose instead that the NFIP charges a risk-based premium in Location F.
A risk-based (actuarially fair) premium is set equal to the average annual flood damage.

  1. Calculate the risk-based premium for Location F.
  2. With this risk-based premium, compute the average annual cost of living in:
    • Location S
    • Location F (assuming the homeowner buys insurance in Location F)
  3. Which location is cheaper on average with a risk-based premium?
  4. How does this compare to your answer in part (b)?


(d) Moral hazard and NFIP policy

Answer in words (no new calculations needed):

  1. Using your results from parts (a)‚Äì(c), explain how subsidized NFIP premiums can create moral hazard in location choices (deciding where to live).
  2. Explain how risk-based premiums (and measures like premium discounts for elevating buildings or floodproofing) could help reduce moral hazard in the NFIP.
  3. Give one concrete policy idea for the NFIP (for example, a rule about rebuilding or premium discounts) that could discourage risky behavior while still helping people manage flood risk.
